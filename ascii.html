ASCII

American Standard Code for Information Interchange

ASCII is a common way computers and the internet understand text. It uses special numbers for each letter, number, and symbol, with a total of 128 unique characters and control codes in standard ASCII-encoded data.

In ASCII encoding, you'll find uppercase and lowercase letters from A to Z, numbers from 0 to 9, and basic punctuation marks. It also includes certain control characters, initially meant for teletype printing terminals, which aren't visible but have specific functions.

ASCII can be represented in many ways. Here are some:

  1. as pairs of hexadecimal digits, which are numbers in base-16 format. This means they can be depicted using the digits 0 through 9, as well as the letters A through F to represent values from 10 to 15 in the decimal system.
  2. as three-digit octal (base 8) numbers
  3. as decimal numbers from 0 to 127
  4. as 7-bit or 8-bit binary
